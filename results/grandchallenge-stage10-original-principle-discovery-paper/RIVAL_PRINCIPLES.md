# Rival Principles

- RRP-01: Accessible Falsifier Boundary - A claim can be stronger when a decisive public falsifier is directly executable, even if some descriptive fields are thin; complete fields without falsifier access remain weak.
- RRP-02: Resolution-Cost Gradient - Missing evidence should cause harsher downgrades when resolving it requires high-cost, unavailable, or unstable execution.
- RRP-03: Executable Specificity Dominance - A narrow executable claim can be safer than a broad well-documented claim if the executable behavior is specific and falsifiable.
- RRP-04: Negative-Control Asymmetry - A failed or decisive negative control should outweigh otherwise favorable fields, replay, or baseline results.
