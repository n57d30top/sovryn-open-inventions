# Paper

## Research question

Can a bounded computational research system refine a measurable principle from corpus anomalies and test it against rivals?

## Candidate principle

The surviving candidate is **Required Evidence Field Boundary**: a claim cannot become stronger than the required evidence fields available to test its mechanism.

## Main result

The principle outperformed two rivals in the Batch 50 discriminating experiments, but it remained bounded. It worked best for benchmark/protocol, dataset reliability, and time-series cases, and only partially transferred to package/runtime checks.
