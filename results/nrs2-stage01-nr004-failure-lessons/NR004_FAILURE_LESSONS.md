# NR-CAND-004 Failure Lessons

NR-CAND-004 failed deep readiness because its evidence remained bounded, meta-level, baseline-vulnerable, and insufficiently inspectable as a new scientific mechanism.

- Receipt-stable replay evidence did not prove nontrivial discovery value
- Simple-rule and bounded-counterexample baselines explained too much
- Known-pattern distance was weak before promotion
- Rival theories strengthened during deep validation
- Counterexample partials repeatedly narrowed the candidate
- Holdout support was mixed rather than decisive
- Replay stability did not remove proof or domain-evidence gaps
- The claim was too meta and process-centered
- External inspectability was possible but not compelling
- The next experiment was not decisive enough
- Bounded formal evidence was easy to overread
- Promotion occurred before gates were hard enough
