# Candidate Theories

- theory-evaluation-fragility: Evaluation Fragility Theory. Benchmark conclusions are fragile when evaluation protocols, splits, metrics, or replay conditions change.
- theory-protocol-sensitivity: Protocol Sensitivity Theory. Benchmark metrics move when source-described split structure is preserved instead of replaced by convenient random splits.
- theory-random-split-inflation: Random-Split Inflation Theory. Convenient random or stratified splits often overstate performance relative to source-described protocols.
- theory-rare-class-metric-illusion: Rare-Class Metric Illusion Theory. Class imbalance and metric choice can mimic protocol or leakage risk.
- theory-protocol-ambiguity-barrier: Protocol Ambiguity Barrier Theory. Ambiguous protocol descriptions cap the strength of benchmark claims even when data loading and modeling succeed.
- theory-replay-robustness: Replay Robustness Theory. Claims deserve higher confidence when replay, fresh seeds, or network-denied execution preserves the conclusion.
- theory-leakage-vs-difficulty-differentiation: Leakage-vs-Difficulty Differentiation Theory. Split deltas must be separated into leakage mechanisms, ordinary protocol difficulty, metric artifacts, or ambiguity.
- theory-tool-value-compression: Tool Value Compression Theory. Custom research tools compress toward packaging, anti-hype, and replay discipline unless they beat simple baselines.
